Gulab Jamun



Ingredients

  • 1/2 tin Nestlé MILKMAID
  • 200 gm Flour
  • 600 gms Grated Paneer
  • 200 gm Sooji
  • 1½ tsp Baking Powder
  • 1½ tsp Baking Soda
  • Oil
  • 2 litres Water
  • 1 kg Sugar
  • 6-8 nos Cardamom

Recipe

  1. Boil sugar and water to prepare syrup remove from fire. Add cardamom and keep aside to cool.
  2. In a mixing bowl put the flour, paneer, sooji, Nestlé MILKMAID, baking powder and baking soda. Mix gently to make a soft dough.
  3. Divide the mixture into 30-35 portions and gently roll into round gulab jamuns. Fry in the oil at very low flame till golden brown in color.
  4. Keep putting the fried gulab jamuns in the prepared sugar syrup. Once all the gulab jamuns are in the sugar syrup bring it to a boil and remove from flame.
  5. Serve warm.

Kaju Katli




Ingredients

  • 1 cup Cashew Nuts
  • 1/2 cup Sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon Cardamom Powder
  • 1/4 cup Water
  • Ghee

Recipe

  1. Grind cashew nuts until smooth powder in a mixer grinder.
  2. Add sugar and water in a pan and cook it over medium flame. Stir continuously and cook until sugar dissolves completely.
  3. Cook until syrup turns thick and little sticky.
  4. Cook and mix continuously until mixture thickens and turns into a big single lump. Turn off the flame and let it cool for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Grease the back side of thali with ghee and transfer prepared mix over it. Grease your palms with ghee and knead it for few minutes. If mixture turns dry, add few drops of milk and knead it to make it smooth.
  6. Roll it out into 1/3 inch thick round shape using a rolling pin.
  7. Cut it using knife into diamond shape pieces. Let it cool for 3-4 minutes.
  8. Separate the pieces and enjoy your favorite Kaju Katli.

Rabri




Ingredients

  • 5 cups milk, full cream
  • 1/2 cup Sugar
  • 4-5 green Cardamoms
  • 12-15 almonds, blanched
  • 2 tbsp pistachios, blanched
  • vark leaves

Recipe

  1. Boil the milk in a wide, heavy-based pan. Add the sugar and cardamom and simmer over low heat.
  2. Do not stir too often, as a layer of cream should form over it. After the layer is formed, push it away from the sides towards the centre, stir the milk below it gently to avoid scorching.
  3. Repeat the process till one-third of the volume of milk is left.
  4. The wider the vessel and the richer the milk, the faster it will thicken.
  5. When done, the colour changes to a beige-cream, and the cream that was pushed aside, collects in layers.
  6. Remove from heat. When cool, transfer to a serving dish.
  7. Garnish with vark leaves(silver leaves) and nuts. Chill and serve.
